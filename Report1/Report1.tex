\begin{abstract}
This report takes the idea that diversification is a vital means for an established company to profit and continue to profit from technology. Particularly, the importance of understanding what makes a business successful and identifying other markets where these strategies are equally advantageous is highlighted. A company that successfully diversifies into areas related to their core competence gain a strategic advantage over their competitors, allowing them access to a new revenue stream. A body of evidence is gathered to support this claim through case studies of established companies. Other diversification strategies are also examined, such as not diversifying at all, or diversifying into areas that are unrelated to the core competence of the business. Through analysis of the performance of several technology firms, this report concludes that related diversification is generally an advantageous strategy for a company to pursue, ensuring that the business remains successful for the years to come.
\end{abstract}
